Recipe for a Painful PhD

Description

Can't find a job after graduation? Think you're super clever? Why not enroll in a PhD program?

Following this recipe will ensure that you have the most painful PhD expeirence ever. As they say, "More suffering equals more prestige!"

Ingredients

  • Genuine passion for a subject
  • Optimism, a growth mindset, and a good attitude.
  • Willingness to see the above slowly deteriorate over many years

Steps

  1. Apply to the most pretegious school you can. Don't worry about fit. So what if the faculty at the Ivy Leage department seem rude and more concerned about obtaining a deanship. It will all be fine once you get there!
  2. In your first years, really try to focus on being the best teacher you can be. Research is important, but teaching skills are what will really get you recognition (and the big buck$
  3. Once you start your dissertation, be sure to work with an advisor who is out of the country for 9 months out of the year. Even better if they don't have email access abroad. Getting feedback is overrated.
  4. If possible, make sure your funding ends before your dissertation is halfway complete. This will force you to choose between taking a full time teaching gig in the middle of rural Pennsylvania where you get paid less than a barista for working.
  5. Wait for the summers to work on your dissertation. You can only make real progress with large chunks of time.
  6. Have a mini-mental break down. Don't take a leave of absense for this: it's better to keep paying dissertation fees so that your committee is motivated to diligently read your drafts.
  7. Do not change your committee members, no matter how bad it gets. A PhD is about passion and love for the subject matter. It will all work out in the end. Somehow…
  8. Repeat steps 5, 6, and 7 until a miracle occurs. Season to taste. Best served rancid and moldy.

DISCLAIMER: The above recipe is satire. Please do not do a PhD for any reason.
